When you learn > How long you learn ?

Not all of us are fit to work with the same performance during the same time of the day. Some of us learn like they’re born to do it in the morning while others do the same at noon or after midnight. Such a difference completely OK, while it is up to us to find out when we learn best.

Consider this: You learn much better at night and despite long study hours during the morning, your short studies during the night time easily outperforms the morning ones. In this case, it is better to shift your focus to the night time to get the best of you. Remember, the point is to get the best of you.

As a final remark, let’s put the case into an equation (trust me, this will help):

X = the duration of your study/learning process

Y= the time of the day during which you study

If we suppose you learn best during the morning hours (1,5 times more than the other times of the day), then 2 hours of study during the morning will turn out to be: 2 hrs(X) * 1,5 (learning performance) = 3 (learning outcome).

On the other side, if you study the same amount during night time (which enables your normal learning performance in this given case), things will be like this:

2 hrs(X) * 1 (learning performance) = 2 (learning outcome).

You see the difference? This is important in two ways. Firstly, it saves you effort and lets you spend your energy on other things. Secondly, it saves you time along with your energy. Go figure it out before you commit yourself to work/studies next time and see the difference yourself.
